The Problem

Wells Fargo app users are not able to organize the money that they saved in their WAY2 SAVINGS account in order to plan more efficiently with their savings.

The Solution

A feature within the Wells Fargo app that allows the users to file the money they have in their savings account into different savings topics if the user desires to.

Forming a Hypothesis

During this past year, I have faced the problem of wanting a budget within my savings for each activity or object I am saving for. As a Wells Fargo app user, I had to create a different way of keeping track of money flow in order to not over spend on one topic.
Before jumping in to any solution, I believe it is important to pinpoint the problem, create a hypothesis, and then test it on users. By testing the hypothesis problem, I am able to understand it more fully and come to a conclusion as to if this problem is a common problem, if it is worth fixing, and an effective solution that users want to use.

My Hypothesis

I believe Wells Fargo app users over spend from their savings account because they are not able to organize the money they have in their savings account into categories.

User Research

When I had developed this hypothesis, I asked myself; who would want to use this feature? What will users feel while using this feature? Will they see a need for it? Where can I get feedback?

Greenhouse by Wells Fargo

During my research, I found the app called greenhouse. This app is designed to help users set aside money for bills, have a spending limit, and keep users on track through reminders. Reviews on this app are very positive. User after user say that they have a hard time budgeting , they like separating their weekly expenses, it brings clarity to their personal finances, they know where their money is going.
This data states that users like to know exactly where their money is and to what purpose it is being used.

User Interviews

User interviews were conducted in order to get a better understand what users would want to see in the new feature and if they would use it. In total, I had 4 interviews. One in person and 3 over the phone. During the interviews I asked questions such as, “Would you like to have a way to categorize your savings?” “Do you already have a system to know what your savings is for?” “Do you already have an app linked to your savings account?” “Do you feel like there would be a benefit to your savings if you had it organized into categories?” “If you had a simple way to organize your savings into categories, would you use it?”

Key Findings

  • Users want to organize their savings so they can remember why they are saving it.
  • Having categories in the savings account helps couples make goals together, and users want that.
  • Users want to organize their savings, but they want it to be easy.

The Opportunity

Based on my research, I am confident that Wells Fargo app users will benefit from organizing their savings account. If we offer the opportunity to Wells Fargo users to organize their savings into categories, users will use it because they like the clarity it brings them.

Exploring Ideas

After getting a better understanding of the problem and the users, I wrote down the ideas that I had in order to fix this problem. I asked, “What if we” and “Why would we” questions to bring new ideas, but to also eliminate ideas that were too far-fetched or off topic.

How Might We Question:

How might Wells Fargo create a way for users to get the most organized, easy to read savings account?
After creating a few solutions, I selected 3 ideas to talk about in more detail.

Idea 1

Link the savings account to a different app that organizes the savings.
Pros
The app would already be set up for exactly what the users are looking for and because it would be a whole app, there would be room to expand and create new ways to organize and or give users more options on how they want to organize.
Cons
We would have to find or make an app that users and Wells Fargo have trust in along with making it easy for the user to link their Wells Fargo app to the other app. Which in the end would probably turn users away because they said they want it to be easy.

Idea 2

Within the savings account, split the amount into categories.
Pros
Wells Fargo would be able to add this feature to their app and create a flow that users are already familiar with in order to organize their savings. The users will trust it because they already trust wells Fargo.
Cons
It could get cluttered on the screens and become confusing.

Idea 3

Create a card to pop up and show users their savings and how they organized it.
Pros
The card would be very clean and only talk about savings organization. The card would be easy to quickly add into the app.
Cons
The potential that user will get confused if the card pops up and they don’t understand it or how to navigate it. The users wouldn’t be able to see their organization in the savings right away.

Why I Chose Idea 2

This idea is the best choice when it comes to creating an enhancement to the app that Wells Fargo will like and users want to use. Some of the reasons:
  • Ease – The product can be created right into what already exists and therefore will have about the same user flow to accomplish a new task.
  • Trust – By keeping the savings organizing in the Wells Fargo app, the users will keep trusting Wells Fargo and will use the new enhancement.
  • Self-Evident – By adding the new options to the user flow, users will be more likely to become curious and learn about organizing their savings.

Ideas to Consider

I do this step in my thought process to help me think of outside factors that I will need to address. These are normally small issues that will arise in the future, but it is better if I can catch them at this point in the process.
  • How will I add these categories into the money transfer process?
  • How will we transfer the money out of the categories?
  • Do we want to show a graph?
  • What kind of graph should we show?
  • How will we create the layout so it isn’t too long or large?
  • Do we want it to be a goal oriented or just a savings organizer?

Questions Answer Breakdown

  • To keep the flow of the app, we will add the savings categories as a drop down the way Wells Fargo already does it with their accounts.
  • Transferring the money out will be the same way that the user transferred it in.
  • Graphs are good, colors and visuals are nice. We do not want to make it a goal app, but just a reflection of the different categories in the savings account.
  • We will use a pie graph to keep the graph from growing too large.

New Considerations

  • Will we let the users choose their colors (right away or as an Easter egg)?
  • Do we want to have percentages or numbers at all on the pie graph?

Sketching

During sketching I played with the idea of cards as an option to see information or putting it directly on the screen. Directly putting it on the screen had a cleaner look and felt like it fit with the Wells Fargo feeling.

Testing and Iterating

After exploring a few different ideas, I did qualitative testing with a few screens to more fully understand what the users prefer. I then mocked up my screens in sketch and used InVision to build my prototype.

Research Findings:

  • Users did not like the title, “Available balance not assigned to Saving Category”. They preferred, “Unassigned balance”.
  • Users did not like the title, “Available balance not assigned to Saving Category”. They preferred, “Unassigned balance”.
  • Users liked that the new screens had the same set up as the Wells Fargo user flow, it made it easy to understand what was happening and what to do next.

Revisiting the Persona and Problem

The new user flow will give Steph (the persona) a more valued experience at Wells Fargo by providing her with the opportunity to organize her savings in hopes of helping her reach her individual savings needs.

Persona Story

Steph just received her biweekly check which was deposited into her checking account the way she prefers it. She goes into her app and hits transfer money. She takes half of her check and puts it towards her house savings.
She’s not quite sure where she wants to put the rest of her money so she puts it into her savings account, but unassigned.
Later that night her fiancé calls her and asks her if she has started planning for the wedding! She lets him know that she’s been thinking about it but hasn’t bought or saved for it. Then she remembers, $900 are sitting in her savings unassigned. She puts her phone on speakers, opens her Wells Fargo app, transfers her money from unassigned balance into a new category, wedding!
Steph then lets her fiancé know that they are actually $900 closer to their dream wedding and she can’t wait to add more to her wedding savings category.
