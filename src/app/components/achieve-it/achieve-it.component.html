Context

Welcome to Achieve It app, a new way to reach your goals:
  • Recognize the progress you are making towards reaching your goals by creating a personalized dream board and setting reminders to keep you on track.
  • Journal through the app about your progress.
  • Play your dreams through our visualize feature. A feature that plays a movie of your chosen pictures and words quickly before your eyes—a scientific way to embed your goals into your subconscious.

Project Scope

  • My role: Interactive design and User Experience
  • Team: Me
  • Timeline: May 2019 (2 weeks)
  • Deliverables: Sketches, mock ups, research findings

The Problem

Goal achievers have lots of choices for goal-oriented apps, but not all the apps are set up in a way where you can put any goal you have in it and achieve it before losing interest or getting discouraged.

The Solution

Achieve It app is equipped with features that help users remember their goals and stay on target such as a “7 Step” program, personalized and required reminders, and a built-in journal—all features that will help with any goal.

Competitive Analysis

In order to reach a hypothesis on what I believe, I completed a thorough competitive analysis. I found that the competitors have fun uplifting apps that make the user feel excited about reaching their goals. Positive criticism about the competitors included image choices—does it fit in the brand and is it too much for the page, too many colors, and the dream boards are not fun to work with because they are bulky and or cannot be moved to fit the user.
Achieve It wants the users to feel excited and happy, but also wants the users to take this opportunity to grow seriously. Positive learning included app tutorials, changing picture positions, and options for the user to be directed to the internet through the app in order to learn more about their goal.
Ideas: Have three main pages; a dream board, a built-in journal, and a visualize option where the images will be flashed for as long as the user chooses in order to help them remember their goals.

My Hypothesis

I believe Achieve It users will reach a higher percentage of reaching their goals because the features this app possesses.

User Flows

Three user flows were created with the end goal in mind. Creating user flows helps me to get the flow of the app started. User flows bring the first pain points to my attention such as needing to add a microphone or video settings to the flow in order to reach the desired outcome of each flow.

Wireframe

With three destination goals previously mapped out through the user flow diagrams, I created my wireframe. A process that helps me more fully visualize how many screens I am going to need to reach each destination and what factors need to be on each screen.

Ideas to Consider

  • What are the important icons that should be in the navigation bar?
  • Do I want to use cards?
  • I need a search bar; how and where do I want to put it?

Sketching

I used Crazy 8’s for my sketching method in order to rework some ideas and screens multiple times quickly. I wanted to display my user flows on paper so that I could get my ideas on what each screen will look like from the home page to the end destination.

Mid-Fidelity Wire Frames and Feedback

I took my crazy 8’s to Sketch and created wireframes that were uploaded into InVision and sent to users for feedback. The Android version was sent to Android 3 Android users and the iOS version was sent to 4 iOS users.

Key Finding

Users thought that the app was clean and easy to use. Android and iOS users thought that the design fit well with the feel of the device they use. However, I find the constructive criticism more useful for the growth of the app.
  • Users said that they: don’t understand the visualization page, don’t know if they would want to share their goals, and they aren’t sure exactly what to do on the Dream Board.
  • My take away: the app needs the option for users to go through a tutorial if they want to and since the users aren’t sure if they want to share their goals —I’m going to leave the share option just in case they want to later.

Style Guide

I created a style guide to keep the product true when creating new features or updating old features. Red was used to symbolize power and blue was used to symbolize loyalty and strength. Typography was chosen off of the style guide that keeps each device true to it’ style guide.

Testing High Fidelity on Users

Frames were updated on InVision and sent out to 2 iOS Users and 2 Android users. Each user got a user story to follow. Their feedback on the most was very positive. The liked the opening message, the option to set reminders, the colors, the shapes, and the directions that help them understand how to use the app, along with the option to go through an app tutorial on the home page.
  • They did not like that there was not more of the app created so they could look through more of the product.

    • The overall concept came together smoothly and fulfills the original problem that users can set a goal and achieve it before they become discouraged or lose interest.
